Maryann Soper BSc (hons) MA

Values-led Digital Lead

Heritage Ability Project Manager

Living Options Devon · Nov 2017 – Aug 2019

As Project Manager for the award‑winning Heritage Ability programme, I led digital accessibility improvements across 23 heritage sites, ensuring disabled visitors could engage with culture, history, and nature through inclusive digital tools and experiences.

  • Designed and implemented digital solutions for disabled visitors across heritage sites of national significance.
  • Delivered accessible digital products including Easy Read guides with 360 videos, British Sign Language videos, and audio tours.
  • Led the “Vote with your Heart” campaign, which won a National Lottery Award.
  • Created an underground mining VR experience for people with disabilities.
  • Managed multi‑site delivery, stakeholder engagement, and accessibility compliance.

Living Options Devon is a leading disability charity with a turnover of over £1m per year. It is a fully funded organisation and relies on the design and delivery of successful projects. I managed the award‑winning Heritage Ability project, delivering digital accessibility improvements across the South West.

  • I implemented a programme of digital accessible solutions across 23 heritage sites in the South West.
  • I researched, wrote, and designed interpretation products for specific disabilities.
  • I gave talks to the public; in person, at conferences and on the radio.
  • I coordinated and hosted a conference in March 2018 in Dorset on ‘Accessible Heritage’.
  • I designed and implemented the ‘Vote with your Heart’ marketing campaign, which won the 2018 National Lottery Awards, Heritage category, by public vote.
  • I reported to the board of trustees and the funders regularly on progress and delivery issues.
  • I managed six members of staff and over 30 volunteers.
  • I used ‘mystery visitor’ feedback and review panels to shape product development.
  • I delivered training to museum and heritage staff on ‘Accessible Visitor Service Design’.
  • I designed and conducted access audits.
  • I created opportunities to gain coverage in magazines, television, and radio.
  • I identified and applied to funders to meet our match‑funding obligations.
  • The project was a finalist in the 2018 Historic England Angel Awards, Interpretation category.
  • I created a sustainable 5‑year business plan.
